What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of defending computers, servers, mobile devices, electronic systems, networks, and data from malicious attacks. It's also known as information technology security or electronic information security.

In our modern daily lives, everything is connected. From banking to socializing, our digital footprint is everywhere. Implementing strong security measures is vital to protect against identity theft, financial loss, or significant data breaches.

Data Privacy

Protecting sensitive information from unauthorized access.

Anonymity

Keeping your online identity and habits safe from tracking.

Common Cyber Threats

Phishing

Deceptive emails or messages designed to trick you into revealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card numbers.

Malware

Malicious software including viruses, worms, and spyware designed to cause damage to a computer, server, or network.

Ransomware

A type of malware that locks and encrypts your data, demanding payment (ransom) to restore your access.

Password Attacks

Attempts by hackers to continuously guess or crack a user's password to gain unauthorized access to an account or system.

Social Engineering

Psychological manipulation of people into performing actions or divulging confidential personal information.

Data Breaches

An incident where secure information is accessed without authorization, leading to exposed confidential data.

Essential Safety Tips

Use Strong Passwords

Combine letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid common dictionary words or easily guessable personal info.

Enable 2FA

Add an extra layer of security to your accounts by requiring a second form of verification.

Avoid Suspicious Links

Don't click links in unsolicited emails or texts. Always verify the source of the message.

Update Regularly

Keep your OS, browsers, and applications updated to regularly patch security vulnerabilities.

Use Antivirus

Install reliable security software and run system scans to intercept threats early.

Never Share OTPs

Never share your One-Time Passwords with anyone, not even official customer support personnel.

Frequently Asked Questions

Phishing is a cybercrime in which a target is contacted by email, telephone, or text message by someone posing as a legitimate institution to lure individuals into providing sensitive data such as personally identifiable information, banking and credit card details, and passwords.

A strong password should be at least 12 characters long and include a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Using a generated passphrase (e.g., "Correct-Horse-Battery-Staple") is often secure and memorable. Use a password manager to keep track of them.

Malware, short for malicious software, refers to any intrusive software developed by cybercriminals (often called hackers) to steal data and damage or destroy computers and computer systems.

With more of our personal and financial information stored online, strong cybersecurity measures are essential to protect against data theft, identity fraud, and severe financial and reputational damage to individuals and organizations.
